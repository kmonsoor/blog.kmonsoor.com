আক্রান্ত। আর এই রোগের প্রভাব শুধু আমাদের ব্যক্তি জীবন না, দাম্পত্য, সন্তান-লালন, চাকরি, ব্যবসা, ধর্ম-কর্ম সবকিছুতেই নেতিবাচক প্রভাব&nbsp;ফেলে। </p> <p>ধরেন, আপনি স্ট্রবেরি ফ্লেভারের আইসক্রিম পছন্দ করেন, তো সাধারণ দোকানে গিয়ে চাইলে ওদের কাছে থাকা দুইটা দামের আছে, আপনি অনেকদিন পরে আইসক্রিম খাচ্ছেন, বেশি দামেরটাই নিলেন। দুই মিনিটে কাজ&nbsp;শেষ।</p> <p>এবার ধরেন, আপনি এমন একটা আইসক্রিমের দোকানে গেলেন, সেখানে হাজারো আইসক্রিমের ফ্লেভার। পনেরো ধরণের স্ট্রবেরি ফ্লেভারের আইসক্রিম, প্রতি ফ্লেভারের আবার কাঠি, কাপ আর কোন তিন ধরণের আছে, তিন দামের। এখন আপনি চিন্তায় পরে গেলেন, সেরা কোনটা হবে, কোনটা খেলে নতুন একটা স্বাদও পাওয়া যাবে, আবার খুব ভালোও লাগবে। ইন্টারনেটে খুঁজলেন &ldquo;best strawberry flavor&rdquo;, দোকানদারকে জিজ্ঞেস করলেন, সে তার নিজের মতামত দিলো, বা সবচেয়ে দামিটার কথা বললো। আপনি দোকানে দাঁড়িয়ে (বা বসে) পনেরো মিনিট ধরে ভাবছেন। এটাই এনালাইসিস প্যরালাইসিস। তারপরে ঘড়ির দিকে তাকিয়ে, নিজের উপরে বিরক্ত হয়ে, হুট-হাট একটা কিনে নিয়ে বের হয়ে&nbsp;গেলেন।</p> <p>অ্যানালাইসিস প্যরালাইসিস (Analysis&nbsp;paralysis)। </p> <hr> <p>নারী, পুরুষ সবাই এই অসুখে ভোগেন। জামাকাপড় কিনতে, ছেলের জন্য পাত্রী দেখতে, আধুনিক উপায়ে &ldquo;ভালবাসার মানুষ&rdquo; খুঁজতে &hellip; &ldquo;ঈশ যদি এই খুঁতটা না থাকতো, সেরম হতো&rdquo;, &ldquo;আচ্ছা, আরেকটু দেখি&rdquo;, &ldquo;যদি বেস্টটা মিস হয়ে যায়?&rdquo; আমার অভিজ্ঞতায় দেখা যে এই অসুখে ভুক্তভোগীরা সবশেষে যেটা বাছাই করে, সেটা ভদ্র ভাষায় বললে &ldquo;না একূলের, না ওকূলের&rdquo;। অথচ, এর আগে তার হাতে অনেক দিক দিয়ে ভালো অনেক অপশন&nbsp;এসেছিলো।</p> <p>এদের শেষ সম্বল দাঁড়ায় &ldquo;তকদিরে ছিলো&rdquo;, &ldquo;কপালে লেখা থাকলে কি করবো&rdquo;, &ldquo;দোকানদাররা এতো বাটপার&rdquo;, &ldquo;দেশ নষ্ট হয়ে গেছে&rdquo;,&nbsp;ইত্যাদি।</p> <p>যাই হোক, এই রোগ থেকে বের হয়ে আসার উপায়&nbsp;কি?</p> <hr> <p>সবার আগে, একটা বিষয় মাথায় রাখা জরুরি। যে রোগ ত্রিশ বছর ধরে আপনার ভেতরে বড় হয়েছে, সেটা আপনি দুই দিনে সারিয়ে তুলতে পারবেন না; It&rsquo;s not an actual living cell. It doesn&rsquo;t breathe, eat, sense or even <strong>reproduce</strong>. The only way it can reproduce is if it hijacks a real living cell&rsquo;s reproduction&nbsp;mechanisms.</p> <p>And that&rsquo;s how all viruses work. They enter your cell, their envelope breaks, and their genetic material is inserted into the cell&rsquo;s genetic material. The cell then &ldquo;thinks&rdquo; that the new genetic material is some important protein that needs to be constantly produced. So that&rsquo;s what it does. It keeps producing it. <strong>The proteins being produced are actually the ones required to create a new virus particle.</strong> They are the envelope + some helper proteins that copy the viral genetic material and put it in the&nbsp;envelopes.</p> <h2 id="what-is-the-cold-virus">What is the cold virus?<a class="headerlink" href="#what-is-the-cold-virus" title="Permanent link">&para;</a></h2> <p>Usually (and in the case of the cold and flu viruses) this process repeats itself until the cell is overflowing with viruses. It&rsquo;s similar to how you get an adrenaline rush when you are in a danger (it&rsquo;s actually the same organs causing it, the adrenal glands). Imagine that your immune system consists of Cops, Emergency Personnel and the National Guard. Well, for all these corps to work, there needs to be a signal that calls them to action. We&rsquo;ll go through each thing your body does as part of the&nbsp;alert:</p> <ul> <li><strong>Fever</strong>: The increase in temperature causes your whole immune system to work better. It&rsquo;s like an alarm goes off and all the firemen start rushing&nbsp;out. </li> <li><strong>Inflammation</strong>: When the cops (white cells on patrol) first find out that virus particles have infiltrated your cells, they immediately go to work and start destroying as many infected cells and particles they can find. At the same time they release a signal which causes the area to swell up and will eventually cause fever. This swelling up speeds up the transfer of reinforcements of Emergency Personel (macrophage white cells - the ones that <span class="caps">EAT</span> intruders) and blocks off the escape of intruders into a different&nbsp;area. </li> <li><strong>Phlegm <span class="amp">&amp;</span> Mucus</strong>: This is another part of the immune alert. Your cells on the respiratory tract produce more phlegm and mucus to help the movement of your cops and emergency personel, to trap intruders trying to escape and force them out, and also to clean up. This conflict isn&rsquo;t &ldquo;bloodless&rdquo;. The casualties are piling up on both sides, and someone needs to get rid of all the carcasses of the dead cells. This is facilitated by phlegm and mucus. It&rsquo;s partly why they are so much more dense when you are sick than&nbsp;normal. </li> <li><strong>Weakness</strong>: Your body can&rsquo;t have you going out and about and wasting energy on useless stuff like solving math problems, lifting weights or feeding your cat. It has a war to fight damn it, and you&rsquo;ll contribute all your energy to it. It&rsquo;s also part of the alert. Your other bodily functions start shutting down. Some can&rsquo;t work because of the fever and inflammation, others are just not important right now. Your body is considering your situation an emergency. Also, the virus might be interfering with their normal functions (although, not so much in a usual case of cold virus). When the fever goes away, then your body will resume normal&nbsp;operations. </li> <li><strong>Bones hurting mildly</strong>: That&rsquo;s partly the fever, and partly the body trying to produce white blood cells. Bone marrow is where all the blood cells are produced, and it swells up slightly during disease to produce more white blood&nbsp;cells. </li> <li><strong>Sleepiness</strong>: See weakness. Your body is trying to dedicate all its resources to removing the&nbsp;intruder. </li> <li><strong><span class="caps">EDIT</span>: Feeling cold and shivers</strong>: This is your body trying to increase the fever even more. The current fever is deemed not enough, and so the reaction is pumped up. As a side-effect you are imagining that you are feeling cold, even though you actually are burning&nbsp;up. </li> </ul> <h2 id="why-is-this-kind-of-infection-so-bad-compared-to-other-infections-like-a-skin-rash">Why is this kind of infection so bad compared to other infections like, a skin rash?<a class="headerlink" href="#why-is-this-kind-of-infection-so-bad-compared-to-other-infections-like-a-skin-rash" title="Permanent link">&para;</a></h2> <p>Because by the time your body realizes you are under attack from a virus, it is already late enough. A skin rash is an early alert, that is executed in a timely and disciplined manner. The alert doesn&rsquo;t even go all the way, your Emergency Personnel are usually enough to take care of it. But when a cold is detected, it&rsquo;s usually when enough viruses have already replicated (incubation period) and are performing <strong>a massive attack</strong> on your body. Your infected cells are bursting simultaneously and in large numbers, infecting even more cells. Your body is in a panic, because the situation is actually quite serious. The enemy can not be allowed to spread further, particularly to somewhere as important as your&nbsp;lungs.</p> <p>It takes so long for your body to get rid of a cold, because it has already taken it long to figure out it&rsquo;s being invaded by a virus. Your body is trying to formulate <strong>antibodies</strong> (the National Guard response), that will go find all your infected cells and put a <strong>hit</strong> on them. I mean a literal hit. Some white cells we call White Killer Cells come along, find all the marked infected cells and induce <strong>apoptosis</strong>, i.e. they cause them to kill themselves, and therefore stop the&nbsp;invasion.</p> <p>Once you start feeling better, is when your body has finally beaten the virus. The fever, sleepiness, weakness, inflammation, phlegm and mucus persist until the area has been cleared and your body makes sure that no more virus particles are&nbsp;around.</p> <h2 id="how-do-i-avoid-catching-a-cold">How do I avoid catching a cold?<a class="headerlink" href="#how-do-i-avoid-catching-a-cold" title="Permanent link">&para;</a></h2> <p>Contrary to popular belief you don&rsquo;t catch a cold just because you went out in cold weather, or you were sweating and some wind happened to blow at that time, or you were walking around barefoot on the cold floor. It&rsquo;s silly when you think about it. How can cold temperature produce a virus out of thin air in the first&nbsp;place?</p> <p>The cold (and flu) virus are flying all around us, and really like snuggling in our respiratory tract. They are usually in <strong>inactive form</strong>. Meaning they are just waiting for the right conditions to start reproducing, by invading your body and hijacking your cells. <strong>Every time you come in contact with another human, it&rsquo;s likely you&rsquo;ll exchange virus particles. But they are inactive, so it&rsquo;s&nbsp;fine.</strong></p> <p>However, <strong>the virus particles will activate if there&rsquo;s a significant drop in temperature</strong>. Particularly at 30-32 C (body normal is 36-37 C), is the perfect temperature for their proteins to start acting up and invade living cells of a host organism. This is why you are more prone to catch a cold if you are feeling cold. Inhaling the cold air in your environment will drop the temperature in your respiratory tract and cause the virus particles to&nbsp;activate.</p> <p>Winter is a nasty period for cold, not just because of the weather, but because we tend to all stay inside with other humans. More contact and breathing the same air over and over, maximizes the chances that you get some virus particles residing in your respiratory tract, waiting for a chance to&nbsp;strike.</p> <p><strong>So, you need to make sure you are somewhere warm, make sure you air the rooms you occupy whenever the outside weather allows, change environments, avoid packed rooms, and always cover up your chest, neck and mouth/nose with warm clothing.
